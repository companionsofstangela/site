  • Who Are the Companions?

    The Congregation of the Companions of St. Angela is a diocesan congregation founded in 1954, at Krugersdorp, which was under the then Diocese of Johannesburg, by †Bishop William Patrick Whelan O.M.I. with the encouragement and support of †Rev. Fr. Jean Vérot O.M.I. The Congregation was found under the patronage of St Angela Merici. The Companions of Saint Angela Merici are different from other religious women as they were founded around Johannesburg, their initial aim was to live and minister to the people in the urban area.

    Like our Patroness, we desire to respond with love to God's call, directing everything to the praise of His Majesty, and showing the world that the kindness and love of God has appeared on Earth. The Sisters make simple vows of Chastity, Poverty and Obedience and observe these Constitutions which are based on the Rule of St. Augustine as interpreted in our Constitution. Through this full and entire Consecration to the following of Christ, we unite ourselves with Him in a life of contemplation and apostolic love.

    We live together in community, sharing in the Eucharist, praising God together, always seeking together to know God's will and sharing in the apostolate entrusted to us by the Church and exercised in her name.

    Who Is St. Angela, Our Patroness?

    Life and Death

    Angela Merici was born on the 21st of March in 1474 on a farm near Desenzano del Garda, a small town on the southwestern shore of Lake Garda in Lombardy, Italy. After living a lengthy life of chastity, teaching and prayer, Angela then died on the 27th of January in 1540, aged 65 at Brescia in the Republic of Venice, Italy. Her body was clothed in the habit of a Fransciscan Tertiary and was interred in the Church of Sant'Afra. The Church of St. Afra was completely destroyed on the 2nd of March 1945 during the Second World War, it was then rebuilt and reopened on the 10th of April 1954. The Church was consecrated on the 27th of January 1956, with a new consecration to St Angela Merici. The Incorrupt body of St Angela now lies at a Shrine known as the Sanctuary of St Angela Merici in Brescia, Italy.

    Work

    St Angela had vision that revealed to her that she was to found an association of virgins who were to devote their lives to the religious training of young girls. This association was a success and she was invited to start another school in the neighboring city, Brescia.

    On 25 November 1055, Merici gathered with 12 young women who had joined in her work in a small house in Brescia near the Church of St Afra, where together they committed themselves in the founding of the Company of St Ursula, placed under the protection of the patroness of medieval universities. Her goal was to elevate family life through the Christian education of future wives and mothers. They were the first teaching order of women religious.She founded the Company of St. Ursula in 1535 in Brescia, in which women dedicated their lives to the service of the Church through the education of girls.

    According to legend, in 1524, while traveling to the Holy Land, Merici became suddenly blind when she was on the island of Crete. Despite this, she continued her journey to the Holy Land and was ostensibly cured of her blindness on her return, while praying before a crucifix, at the same place where she was struck with blindness a few weeks before.

    Sainthood

    St Angela was beatified on the 30th of April 1768 at Rome by Pope Clement XIII, she was then Canonized on the 24th of May 1807 at Rome by Pope Pius VII and her feast day is celebrated on the 1st of June. She is the patroness of sicknesses, handicapped people, loss of parents, courage and strength.
